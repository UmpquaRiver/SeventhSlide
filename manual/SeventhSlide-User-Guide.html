About this guide. This manual explains everything you need to run SeventhSlide for a service: from preparing songs ahead of time to controlling the screens live. You do not need any prior experience with presentation software. Read Chapter 1 first; after that, each chapter stands on its own.

Throughout the guide, the names of on-screen buttons and panels are set in small capitals, and keys you press look like this.

Getting Started

A contemporary worship service requires an effective way to display lyrics, scripture, images, and videos across projectors, reinforcement displays, foldback displays, live stream graphics, and more. Finding a software to manage all these sources and destinations, while also producing a tasteful output, is difficult. Some offerings exist, but they tend to be either needlessly expensive subscriptions or are feature starved, rendering them obsolete. SeventhSlide aims to meet all these needs for free while remaining unintimidating for the end user by relying on one intuitive control window.

In SeventhSlide, you can prepare ahead of time by collecting the songs, scripture references, and announcements you will need into a service, an ordered list like a printed order of worship. When the worship service begins, you click through that list and SeventhSlide sends each line to the screens. In cases which require more spontaneity, such as a youth group meeting or camping event, SeventhSlide is also capable of loading media on the fly. Either approach is ready to use depending on your needs.

Starting the program

  1. Double-click the SeventhSlide icon. After a moment the control window opens by itself.
  2. Connect your projector or second screen as you normally would in the operating system (extend the desktop, do not mirror it).
  3. You are ready. Everything below happens inside the one window.
Note

SeventhSlide also serves its control window to a web browser. On another device on the same network, open http://this-computer:49777/admin, using the host computer's network address. This is handy if you prefer to operate from a tablet or other separate device on the same network.

The control window at a glance

The window is divided into three columns. You will spend almost all of your time moving between them, so it is worth learning what each one does.

The left column: Service & Library
The top half holds the Service: the running order for this service. The bottom half is the Library, your whole collection of songs, Bibles, announcements, images and videos, arranged on tabs.
The center column: Controller
When you pick something from the service, its slides appear here. This is where you move from line to line during the service, and where the Blank and Freeze buttons live.
The right column: Outputs
A small live picture of each screen you are driving (for example a main projector and a stage monitor), so you can always see exactly what your congregation sees.
Tip

The picture on the right updates the instant you change anything. If you are ever unsure what is on the screens, glance at the right column. It never lies.

Building a Service

A service is simply your order of worship: the songs, readings and notices for one service, listed in the order you will use them. You build it in the top-left panel, usually before the service begins.

Choosing or creating a service

At the top of the left column is the Service menu. Click the wide button (it shows the current service name) to open the list.

  • To open a service, click its name.
  • To create one, type a name in the box at the bottom (for example, “Sabbath Worship - June 7”) and click +.
  • To rename or delete a service, use the pencil and × icons that appear on its row.
  • No Service clears the running order without deleting anything.
  • + Group makes a folder, so you can keep the several services of a weekend (Friday Night, Sabbath Morning, and so on) together.

Adding items to the service

Everything you place in a service comes from the Library in the lower-left panel. Each item in the Library has a small green + button. Clicking it adds that item to the end of the current service.

You can add songs, Bible passages, announcements, images and videos this way. Chapters 3 through 7 explain each kind of material in turn.

Reordering and removing

  • Reorder: drag an item up or down by its dotted handle.
  • Remove: click the red × on its row. This removes it from the service only; the original stays safe in your Library.

Folders and dividers

The + button beside the service name opens a small menu:

  • Add folder: groups a set of images together inside the running order.
  • Add divider: inserts a labelled separator (for example “Sermon” or “Offering”) to mark the sections of your service.
Note

The gear ⚙ beside the service name opens Service Options, where you can choose a different look for this particular service. That is covered in Chapter 9.

The Song Library

The Library is your permanent collection. Songs you add here stay available for every future service, so most of your preparation is done once and then simply reused.

Finding a song

Open the Songs tab in the lower-left panel. Type any part of a title or author into the search box and the list narrows as you type.

Adding a new song

  1. Click New Song. The song editor opens.
  2. Fill in the Title and one or more Authors.
  3. If you wish, add the Songbook name and entry number (for hymns), and the copyright, CCLI number and musical key.
  4. Type or paste the words in the Lyrics area, then click Save.

Guided and Raw modes

The lyrics area has two modes, chosen by the toggle at its top-right:

Guided
The recommended way. Each section of the song (Verse 1, Chorus, and so on) is a separate labelled box. Use the green + to add a section. SeventhSlide breaks long sections into screen-sized slides for you.
Raw XML
For advanced users importing or hand-editing the underlying song file.

Importing existing songs

If you already have songs in the common .xml song format (such as OpenLyrics files exported from other programs), click Import, then choose one or more files. They are added to your Library at once.

Tip

To add several songs to a service quickly, tick the box on each song row to select them, then use Add to Service in the bar that appears at the bottom of the list.

Important

The red × on a Library row deletes the song from your collection permanently. To take a song out of the running order without deleting it, use the red × in the Service panel instead (Chapter 2).

Going Live: The Controller

The Controller, in the center of the window, is where you work during the service. Whatever you do here appears on the screens straight away, so this is the chapter to be comfortable with before your first service.

Putting an item on screen

Click any item in the Service panel. Its contents open in the Controller as a list of lines, one for each thing that can be shown on screen. This list is sometimes called the slide map.

The small labels down the left edge tell you what each line is:

T
the title slide.
V1, V2…
verses, in order.
C
a chorus.
B
a bridge.

Moving from line to line

There are three ways to advance, and you may mix them freely:

  • Click any line in the slide map to jump straight to it.
  • Use the Previous and Next buttons beneath the slide map.
  • Press the → / ↓ / Page Down keys to go forward, and ← / ↑ / Page Up to go back.
Tip

A remote presentation clicker that sends Page Up and Page Down will drive SeventhSlide perfectly. This is handy for presenters wishing to control their own slides.

Blank and Freeze

Two buttons let you take command of the screens at any moment:

Blank
Hides the words and shows a plain background (or black). Click it again to bring the words back. Use it during prayer, or whenever the screen should rest.
Freeze
Holds the current picture on the screens while you get the next item ready behind the scenes. Your congregation keeps seeing the frozen frame; nothing you do in the Controller reaches the screens until you unfreeze.

These two buttons act on all of your screens at once. (To blank or freeze a single screen on its own, see Chapter 8.)

Shortcut

Press F to freeze or unfreeze every screen, and B to blank or unblank, without reaching for the mouse. Press the same key again to return to normal. (These keys are ignored while you are typing in a box, so they won’t get in your way.)

Advancing automatically

For an unattended slideshow (a pre-service loop of announcements, say), turn on Auto-advance using the play-shaped switch at the top of the Controller. The gear beside it opens the timing options.

Interval
how many seconds each slide stays on screen.
Loop
return to the beginning when the end is reached.
Through items
continue past the end of one item into the next.
Stop at dividers
pause when a divider is reached.
Auto-scroll
keep the live line in view as the map advances.

Scripture

SeventhSlide can display Bible passages directly, without your having to type them out. Choose the translation, the book, the chapter and the range of verses, and the passage is ready to project.

Selecting a passage

Open the Bibles tab in the lower-left panel.

  1. Choose a translation from the top drop-down.
  2. Choose a book, then a chapter.
  3. Set the verse range: a starting verse, “to”, and an ending verse.
  4. Click Go to put the passage on screen at once, or the + to add it to your service for later.

The verses of the chosen chapter are listed below, so you can read them before deciding on a range.

Adding more translations

Use the small + beside the translation drop-down to import a Bible in .xml format. The pencil renames a translation and the red × removes one.

Note

Once a passage is in your service, it behaves like any other item: select it in the Controller and step through the verses with Next and Previous, just as you would a song.

Announcements

Announcements are short notices (a welcome, a reminder, a theme for the day) that you compose once and reuse. Each one can follow a chosen layout so your notices look consistent.

Creating an announcement

Open the Announce tab in the lower-left panel and click + New Announcement. Give it a title and its text, choose a layout, and save.

Each announcement row works like every other Library item:

  • green + adds it to the current service;
  • pencil edits its wording or layout;
  • red × deletes it from the Library.
Note

The layout of an announcement, where the text sits and how it looks, is defined by a template. You can create and adjust these templates from a screen’s settings; see Chapter 9.

Images & Video

Photographs and video clips are added to your Library and can then be used in a service just like songs. Common service uses include slide presentations imported from PowerPoint, Keynote, LibreOffice Impress, or other software that supports image export; Mission Spotlight videos; and the general display of graphics and visual media.

Images

Open the Images tab. Click Upload Images to bring pictures in from your computer; you may select several at once.

  • green + adds the picture to your service;
  • red × deletes it from the Library.
  • New Folder groups pictures (for example, a set of photos for one item) so they travel together.

Video

Open the Videos tab and click Upload Video. Once a video is in your service and selected in the Controller, play controls appear:

  • Play and Pause to control playback;
  • Blank and Freeze, just as for any other item.
Tip

For a looping welcome slideshow, add your images (or a folder of them) to the service, then turn on Auto-advance with Loop switched on (Chapter 4).

Screens & Outputs

An output is one destination for your presentation: a projector, a television, a stage monitor, or a feed for the livestream. SeventhSlide can drive several at the same time, each with its own look, and the right-hand column shows you a live picture of every one.

The Outputs panel

Across the top of each output’s thumbnail are three buttons that affect that screen alone:

Ignore
The controller stops accounting for this output and does not display any text. Useful when an output is not in use and dividing lines to it is unnecessary.
Freeze
Holds this one screen’s current frame.
Blank
Hides this one screen’s content.

Remember the difference: the Blank and Freeze buttons in the center Controller act on every screen; these act on a single screen.

Sending an output to a monitor

Beneath each output’s thumbnail is a small strip for placing that output on a physical monitor, such as your projector or a stage television.

  1. Choose a monitor from the drop-down. Each connected display is listed, and the line below the drop-down shows the one you picked together with its size (for example, 3840×2160).
  2. Click Send. The output fills that monitor at once, and the button changes to Stop.
  3. Click Stop to take the output back off the monitor.

SeventhSlide remembers these choices, so the same outputs return to the same monitors the next time you start the program. If a monitor is unplugged its output simply waits, and reappears when the monitor is connected again.

Sound on a screen

Beside the Send button is a small speaker button. It controls whether the sound of a video plays through that monitor:

  • a loud-speaker 🔊 means the sound is on;
  • a crossed-out speaker 🔇 means that screen is muted.

Because the local computer will likely drive computer audio for the main speaker mix in the church/venue, it is important to manage which outputs drive sound out of the local computer. Leaving all outputs enabled might mean duplicate audio output, highly unideal for live use. Because of this, muting a screen here affects only this computer’s own output; a web browser or browser source watching the same output is left playing as before.

Note

This strip appears only in the SeventhSlide desktop program, which is the part that can place windows on particular monitors. When you operate from a web browser instead (see Chapter 1), the screens are driven by whichever computer the browser tab is open on.

Adding and editing outputs

Click ⚙ Settings at the top of the right column. The list of outputs appears, where you can reorder them, edit them, delete them, or click + Add Output to create a new one.

The General tab holds the settings that belong to the screen itself:

Output Name
a label such as “Main”, “Lower-Thirds”, or “Foldback”.
Canvas Width & Height
the pixel size of the screen, usually 1920×1080 for a modern projector or television.
What this output shows
tick which content types (video, images, announcements) appear on this screen.
Exempt from Global Blank / Freeze
keep this screen live even when you press the central Blank or Freeze; ideal for a stage monitor that the musicians must always be able to read.
Note

How each output looks (its fonts, colors, background and the position of the words) is not set here. That lives in themes, the subject of the next chapter.

Themes & Appearance

A theme is a saved look. By keeping appearance separate from content, SeventhSlide lets you design a style once and then apply it to any song, reading or service, and change it everywhere at the press of a button.

There are two kinds of theme, and they combine:

Text themes
Everything except the background: the font, its size, where the words sit, the colors, and how slides change.
Background themes
Just the backdrop: a color, or an image.

Where themes live

Open an output (⚙ Settings → Edit) and choose the Themes tab.

The panel at the top, Default themes per category, sets which theme is used for each kind of content unless something overrides it. Below that are your lists of text themes and background themes; each has Edit, Dup (duplicate) and Del buttons, and + Add buttons to create new ones.

Editing a text theme

Click Edit on a text theme to open its settings, which are arranged on tabs. A live Canvas Preview at the foot of the dialog shows your changes as you make them.

Layout
the position and size of the text box, the alignment of the words, and an optional small verse indicator.
Text
the font family and size, and the colors of the words.
Behavior
how slides transition: fades, fluid slides, and how many lines to show at once.
Bible
separate styling for Scripture: where the reference and the verse text appear, and whether to show verse numbers.
Copyright
the placement of the copyright / CCLI line.
Media
the area used by video and images.

Editing a background theme

A background theme has a single Background tab: choose a solid color, or pick an image to fill the screen behind the words.

A different look for one service

Sometimes a single event needs its own styling (a special evangelistic series, perhaps). Open the ⚙ gear beside the service name to reach Service Options, and choose which theme each output should use for this service only. Other services are untouched.

Tip

Different screens can wear different themes at the same time. The example used throughout this guide drives a full-screen Main output, a Lower-Thirds caption bar for the livestream, and a plain Foldback monitor for the platform presenters.

Program Settings

A few settings apply to the program as a whole. You will normally set these once, when you first install SeventhSlide, and rarely touch them again.

Click ⚙ Settings at the top of the right-hand column to open the settings dialog. (This is the same dialog that lists your outputs, from Chapter 8.)

CCLI License Number
Your organization’s copyright-reporting number. When set, it is printed on the screen alongside a song’s copyright line.
Bundle local fonts with outputs
Copies the fonts you use to the screens, so that type looks identical even when an output is shown on a different computer. Leave this on unless you have a reason not to.
Admin Preview Video
Controls how video appears in the small preview thumbnails on the right. Still Frame is recommended: it shows a paused frame and uses very little of the computer’s power.

Click Save to keep your changes, then Close.

Before a service: a short checklist

  1. Open or create the service for today.
  2. Add the songs, readings, announcements and media you need, in order.
  3. Confirm the projector or screen is connected and extended.
  4. Check the right-hand column shows the correct picture on each output.
  5. Select the first item, and you are ready to begin.
